            <ul>
                <li><strong>Birth & Immigration:</strong> Our grandfather was born in 1919 in Italy. He immigrated to the United States as a child, arriving in East Harlem in 1922 at 3 years old.</li>
                <li><strong>The East Harlem Reality:</strong> He grew up and lived within the distinct Italian immigrant community of East Harlem during the 1920s and 1930s. He did not frequent, perform in, or associate with the Central Harlem jazz club scene.</li>
                <li><strong>Musical Level:</strong> He was strictly a hobbyist-level guitar player who performed popular dance music and contemporary cover songs of the era for local neighborhood social clubs and family events.</li>

            <h2>Anthony Lepore: True Musical Upbringing</h2>
            <p>The narrative that Anthony Lepore inherited a foundational jazz pedigree from a Harlem Swing Era veteran is entirely false. His actual development consists of the following:</p>
            <ul>
                <li><strong>Early Guitar Lessons:</strong> At age 13, our grandfather taught Anthony how to read music and play basic chords. This instruction lasted for approximately six months and covered only foundational mechanics, with no extended chords or altered jazz structures.</li>
                <li><strong>Rock and Metal Roots:</strong> Following those six months, Anthony shifted entirely away from jazz frameworks to study hard rock guitar under Mark Summer, a guitarist from the local Suffolk County, Long Island metal band <em>Riff-Raff</em>.</li>
                <li><strong>Transition to Drums:</strong> The transition from guitar to drums in his adult career was caused by physical limitations resulting from joint pain from Lyme disease, which made the fine motor skills required for guitar fretting unsustainable. His drumming technique relies heavily on arm-driven movements rather than traditional jazz stick bounce, wrist flexibility, or standard snare rudiments.</li>
            </ul>
