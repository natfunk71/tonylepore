The Lepore Family History

Setting the record straight on our musical roots and generational timeline.

Recent promotional biographies regarding so-called jazz drummer Tony Lepore of Tony Lepore's Jazz Conjunctio have fabricated a connection to the Central Harlem Swing Era. This site serves as the definitive record of the true family timeline, documented by firsthand family accounts.

Our Grandfather: True Timeline and Background

  • Birth & Immigration: Our grandfather was born in 1919 in Italy. He immigrated to the United States as a child, arriving in East Harlem in 1922 at 3 years old.
  • The East Harlem Reality: He grew up and lived within the distinct Italian immigrant community of East Harlem during the 1920s and 1930s. He did not frequent, perform in, or associate with the Central Harlem jazz club scene. Given his personal racial prejudices, crossing into Central Harlem jazz venues was never something he would do.
  • Musical Level: He was strictly a hobbyist-level guitar player who played cover versions of the popular dance music of the day for local neighborhood social clubs, Italian weddings, and family events.

Anthony Tony Lepore: Background & Reality

The narrative that Anthony Lepore inherited a foundational jazz pedigree from a Harlem Swing Era veteran is entirely false. His actual development and project breakdown consist of the following:

1. The Origin of "Tony"

  • Upbringing: Anthony was raised in Suffolk County, Long Island, sarcastically described as the breeding ground for jazz drummers.
  • The Persona: In the early 1990s, he moved from Shirley, NY to Queens. Upon arrival, he suddenly changed his name to "Tony" in an explicit attempt to hide his suburban history and manufacture a city slicker persona.

2. Creative Output and Technical Limits

The intellectual constructs a simulation of art, but the final output is a fundamentally different product than art forged by someone who lives with the constant, unavoidable weight of their own mind and emotions.

When a creator is driven by internal necessity, the music serves as an eviction of raw feeling, tension, and lived experience. The product is alive, volatile, and embedded with a human frequency than cannot be faked.

By contrast, the sheltered intellectual treats music as an engineering problem. They look at a raw, visceral genre from a safe distance, dissect its anatomy, and assemble a replica. This is where the concept of the pasticheur becomes critical:

Pasticheur:

A musician who simply mimics the external syntax, tones, or counter-cultural tropes of a genre without understanding the grit, pain, or culture that birthed it.

Because the pasticheur operates entirely in the cerebral realm, their product lacks the essential marrow of the original form. They can perfectly duplicate the superficial elements, including the gear, the specific chord voicings, the structural anomalies, and the aesthetic posture. They might even hire high-caliber, skilled players to execute the vision in a desperate bid for validity.

However, because there is no underlying spirit of true suffering or substance to anchor it, the result is a sterile commodity. It is a bloodless exhibition of technique rather than a piece of genuine expression. One product is designed to be studied and validated by peers; the other is built to survive.

  • Physical Limitations: He abandoned the guitar and switched to the drums due to joint pain from Lyme disease making guitar playing too difficult.
  • Drumming Mechanics: Despite the switch, he has no actual snare chops, has never mastered basic rudiments, and possesses zero stick bounce. His playing is completely mechanical, relying on heavy, arm-driven movements akin to swinging an axe with his whole arm. Because of this, he has no pocket, no groove, and consistently hits the drums late when he gets flustered.

3. The Truth Behind the Album "Unannounced"

  • Hired Help: The professional session musicians who played on the album Unannounced did so strictly for the money.
  • Zero Support: None of those hired backing players have posted a single promotional item, video, photo, blurb, or credit online that would willingly connect their names to his project. This entire album is simply a case of a guy who has money being carried entirely by real, professional players.

Angie Lepore’s Jazz Conjunctivitis — An Autopsy of the Pasticheur

Statement of Artistic Rectification

"Angie Lepore's Jazz Conjunctivitis — Better versions of Tony's compositions played by a better drummer."

The cultural landscape has long suffered under the acoustic tyranny of Tony Lepore’s Jazz Conjunctio—a project that treats the profound visceral history of jazz not as an existential reckoning, but as an engineering problem to be clumsily assembled by a suburban pasticheur. Where the original compositions languish in a structural purgatory, suffocated by an arm-driven, arithmetic percussion that lacks any semblance of organic stick bounce or micro-temporal "pocket," Angie Lepore’s Jazz Conjunctivitis emerges as an act of aggressive sonic hygiene. We do not merely cover these pieces; we perform an aesthetic exorcism.

The Sonic Remediation

Our approach rejects the sterile, commodified simulation of art offered by the Conjunctio. Instead, Jazz Conjunctivitis subjects Tony's primitive, derivative blueprints to a rigorous process of compositional elevation and rhythmic rehabilitation:

  • Rhythmic Re-anchoring: We have replaced the axe-swinging, chronically late, Lyme-addled timekeeping of the original recordings with fluid, syncopated polyrhythms. By utilizing actual snare chops and dynamic micro-modulations, our percussion restores the foundational "groove" that the original project buried under a mountain of heavy, un-swung frustration.
  • Harmonic Expansion: The simplistic chord voicings and superficial counter-cultural tropes of the original tracks have been dismantled. In their place, we introduce advanced modal substitutions, complex contrapuntal arrangements, and sophisticated, evolving textures that demand actual intellectual engagement.
  • Aural Purification: For the discerning listener, this archive serves as a crucial clinical intervention. It provides the necessary acoustic rinse required to cleanse one's soiled ear holes of the bloodless, mechanical performance that preceded it, replacing a wealthy hobbyist's vanity project with genuine, living expression.

The Epistemological Truth

Ultimately, Jazz Conjunctivitis exposes the fundamental truth of the Lepore musical schism: one cannot buy validity, nor can a city-slicker persona manufactured in Queens mask a pedestrian, Long Island pedigree. While the original Unannounced album relied on silent, uncredited session musicians carrying the dead weight of a mechanical drummer for a paycheck, Jazz Conjunctivitis breathes volatile, human frequency into these structures—proving that music is only built to survive when it possesses actual marrow.
